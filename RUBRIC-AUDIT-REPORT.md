#### 3.7 The Effects of Diet/Nutrition ✅

**Status:** ✅ **FULLY COMPLIANT**

**Coverage:**
- **Slide 10** - Diet, Nutrition & Food Access:
  - ✅ Traditional diabetes-protective diet: lean proteins (wild game, fish), Three Sisters (corn, beans, squash), berries, nuts, vegetables
  - ✅ Forced dietary transition: government commodity foods (refined flour, sugar, lard)
  - ✅ Frybread origin from commodity rations (fuels diabetes epidemic)
  - ✅ Food deserts: 76.7% on reservations live >1 mile from grocery store vs. 41.2% general U.S. (Wedekind et al., 2021)
  - ✅ Poverty: Nearly 50% have income ≤200% of federal poverty level (Wedekind et al., 2021)
  - ✅ Limited fresh produce availability
  - ✅ Lactose intolerance affecting dairy recommendations
  - ✅ My Native Plate program (SDPI 2022)
  - ✅ Nursing strategies: frame as returning to ancestral foods, involve family in meal planning, acknowledge access challenges
  - ✅ Visual: My Native Plate program image

#### 3.8 Special Considerations ✅

**Status:** ✅ **FULLY COMPLIANT**

**Coverage:**
- **Slide 11** - Communication & Special Considerations:
  - ✅ Communication styles: limited eye contact shows respect, silence = thoughtful processing, "yes" may mean "I hear you"
  - ✅ Storytelling preferred, humor as coping mechanism
  - ✅ Historical trauma: boarding schools, unethical medical experiments → mistrust (Gone & Trimble, 2012; Walls & Whitbeck, 2012)
  - ✅ Trust-building: acknowledge trauma, transparency
  - ✅ Indian Time: flexible, present-focused orientation (adapt scheduling)
  - ✅ Geographic barriers: limited transportation, long distances to IHS clinics, financial constraints
  - ✅ Solutions: telehealth, coordinate with IHS, involve Community Health Representatives, talking circles, SDPI resources
  - ✅ Trust building: keep promises, be transparent, own mistakes
  - ✅ Visual: Elder in hospital with family members
