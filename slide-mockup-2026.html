Diabetes Prevalence Among AI/AN Adults

American Indians and Alaska Natives face a staggering diabetes burden. The prevalence of diagnosed diabetes among AI/AN adults is 13.6%, compared to just 7% among Caucasians—making them 1.5 times more likely to have diabetes.

Complications

Disproportionate Impact

2.3× more likely to die from diabetes-related complications
Nearly double the amputation rate compared to general population
Significantly higher kidney failure rates requiring dialysis
Elevated rates of diabetic retinopathy and vision loss

Rural Native American men are particularly affected, with an 18.9% prevalence rate compared to 13.7% in urban areas. These statistics represent real people, real families, and an urgent need for culturally competent diabetes care.
